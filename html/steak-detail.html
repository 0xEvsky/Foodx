Perfect Pan-Seared Steak

Prep Time: 30 mins Cook Time: 10 mins Servings: 2 Cuisine: American
Dinner Beef Quick Special Occasion

Overview

Learn how to cook the perfect steak at home with this foolproof pan-searing method. This recipe produces a steak with a beautiful crust on the outside while maintaining a juicy, tender interior cooked to your preferred doneness. Perfect for a special dinner or when you're craving restaurant-quality results at home.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 2 Ribeye or New York strip steaks (1½-inch thick, about 1 pound each)
  • 2 tbsp Vegetable or canola oil
  • 3 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves Garlic, lightly crushed
  • 2 sprigs Fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs Fresh thyme

Seasoning

  • 1 tbsp Coarse kosher salt
  • 2 tsp Freshly ground black pepper

Optional Garnish

  • To taste Flaky sea salt (such as Maldon)
  • As needed Fresh herbs for garnish

Instructions

1

Prepare the Steak

Remove the steaks from the refrigerator at least 30 minutes before cooking to allow them to come to room temperature. This helps the steak cook more evenly. Pat the steaks dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture, which will help develop a better crust.

2

Season Generously

Just before cooking, season both sides of the steaks generously with kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, pressing the seasonings into the meat to help them adhere.

3

Heat the Pan

Heat a cast-iron skillet or heavy-bottomed pan over high heat for about 5 minutes until very hot. The pan should be smoking slightly before you add the oil.

4

Sear the Steaks

Add the vegetable oil to the hot pan and swirl to coat. Carefully place the steaks in the pan, laying them away from you to prevent oil splatter. Cook undisturbed for 3-4 minutes until a deep brown crust forms.

5

Flip and Add Aromatics

Flip the steaks and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes. Reduce heat to medium. Add the butter, garlic cloves, and herb sprigs to the pan. As the butter melts, tilt the pan slightly and use a spoon to baste the steaks continuously with the butter for about 1-2 minutes.

6

Check Doneness

For medium-rare, cook until the internal temperature reaches 130-135°F (54-57°C) when tested with an instant-read thermometer. For medium, aim for 135-145°F (57-63°C). Remember that the temperature will rise about 5 degrees during resting.

7

Rest the Steak

Transfer the steaks to a cutting board or warm plate. Loosely tent with foil and let rest for at least 5-10 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, resulting in a more tender, juicy steak.

8

Serve

Slice the steak against the grain or serve whole. If desired, spoon some of the reserved pan juices over the steak and sprinkle with flaky sea salt just before serving. Garnish with fresh herbs if using.

Chef's Tips

Steak Selection

Choose steaks that are at least 1½ inches thick for best results. Ribeye, New York strip, and filet mignon are excellent choices for pan-searing due to their tenderness and marbling.

Temperature Guide

For perfect doneness: Rare: 120-125°F (49-52°C); Medium-Rare: 130-135°F (54-57°C); Medium: 135-145°F (57-63°C); Medium-Well: 145-155°F (63-68°C); Well Done: 155°F+ (68°C+).

Resting Is Essential

Never skip the resting period. It's as important as the cooking itself. A good rule of thumb is to rest the steak for about half the time it took to cook.
