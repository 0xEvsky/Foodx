Homemade Ramen

Prep Time: 30 mins Cook Time: 3 hours Servings: 4 Cuisine: Japanese
Japanese Soup Comfort Food Noodles

Overview

This homemade ramen recipe features a rich, flavorful broth with tender pork, fresh noodles, and traditional toppings. While it takes time to develop the deep flavors in the broth, the result is well worth the effort. This authentic Japanese favorite will transport your taste buds straight to Tokyo.

Ingredients

Broth

  • 2 lbs Pork bones (neck bones or trotters)
  • 2 Chicken carcasses or 2 lbs chicken wings
  • 2 Onions, halved
  • 2 Carrots, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves Garlic, smashed
  • 2-inch piece Ginger, sliced
  • 2 tbsp Vegetable oil
  • ¼ cup Soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp Mirin

Toppings and Assembly

  • 1 lb Fresh ramen noodles
  • ½ lb Pork belly, sliced
  • 4 Soft-boiled eggs, halved
  • 4 Green onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 sheets Nori (seaweed), cut into strips
  • 1 cup Bean sprouts
  • To taste Chili oil or togarashi

Instructions

1

Prepare the Broth

Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C). Place pork bones, chicken, onions, carrots, garlic, and ginger on a baking sheet. Drizzle with oil and roast for 30-40 minutes until well browned, turning occasionally.

2

Simmer the Broth

Transfer the roasted ingredients to a large stockpot. Add 4 quarts of water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 2-3 hours, skimming off any foam that rises to the surface. Add soy sauce and mirin in the last 30 minutes.

3

Prepare the Pork Belly

While the broth simmers, cook the pork belly in a separate pot of water with 2 tbsp soy sauce and 1 tbsp sugar for about 1 hour until tender. Let cool, then slice thinly.

4

Prepare the Eggs

Bring a pot of water to a boil. Carefully add eggs and cook for exactly 6 minutes. Transfer to an ice bath, then peel when cool enough to handle.

5

Strain the Broth

After 2-3 hours, strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean pot. Discard solids and keep the broth hot.

6

Cook the Noodles

Cook ramen noodles according to package instructions until al dente. Drain well.

7

Assemble the Ramen

Divide noodles among four bowls. Ladle hot broth over noodles. Top each bowl with sliced pork belly, halved egg, green onions, nori, bean sprouts, and chili oil. Serve immediately.

Chef's Tips

Broth Clarity

For a clearer broth, avoid boiling vigorously; maintain a gentle simmer throughout cooking.

Time-Saving Option

Use a pressure cooker to reduce broth cooking time to 1 hour while maintaining rich flavor.

Storage

The broth freezes well for up to 3 months. Store toppings separately for quick meals later.
