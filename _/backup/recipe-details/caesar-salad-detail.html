Classic Caesar Salad

Prep Time: 20 mins Cook Time: 10 mins Servings: 4 Cuisine: Italian-American
Salad Quick Lunch Dinner

Overview

This classic Caesar salad features crisp romaine lettuce, homemade croutons, and a rich, creamy dressing with the perfect balance of garlic, anchovy, and Parmesan flavors. The perfect side dish or light meal that's sure to impress with its restaurant-quality taste and presentation.

Ingredients

Salad Base

  • 2 heads Romaine lettuce, washed and dried
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, freshly grated
  • 2 cups Croutons (homemade or store-bought)

Homemade Croutons

  • 4 slices Day-old bread, cubed
  • 3 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1/4 tsp Garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp Italian seasoning
  • To taste Salt and pepper

Caesar Dressing

  • 1 Egg yolk, room temperature
  • 2 Anchovy fillets, minced (or 1 tsp anchovy paste)
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp Fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/3 cup Extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, freshly grated
  • To taste Freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

1

Make the Croutons

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Toss bread cubes with olive oil, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes until golden and crisp, tossing halfway through. Set aside to cool.

2

Prepare the Dressing

In a medium bowl, whisk together the egg yolk, minced anchovies, garlic, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, and Worcestershire sauce. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while whisking continuously to emulsify. Stir in the grated Parmesan and black pepper.

3

Prepare the Lettuce

Tear romaine lettuce into bite-sized pieces and place in a large salad bowl. Make sure the lettuce is thoroughly dried to allow the dressing to adhere properly.

4

Assemble the Salad

Add enough dressing to the lettuce to coat lightly and toss well. Add croutons and additional Parmesan cheese. Toss gently to combine. Serve immediately on chilled plates with extra Parmesan and black pepper on top.

Chef's Tips

Raw Egg Safety

If you're concerned about using raw egg, you can substitute with 2 tablespoons of mayonnaise or use pasteurized eggs.

Variations

Add grilled chicken, shrimp, or salmon to make it a complete meal. For a vegetarian version, omit the anchovies and use capers instead.

Make Ahead

The dressing can be made up to 2 days ahead and stored in the refrigerator. Just bring to room temperature and whisk before using.
