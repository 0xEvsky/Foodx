Classic Fried Rice

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 15 mins Servings: 4 Cuisine: Asian
Quick Easy Weeknight Rice

Overview

A quick and easy classic fried rice recipe that's perfect for using up leftover rice. This versatile dish can be customized with your favorite protein (chicken, shrimp, tofu) and vegetables. A delicious and satisfying meal ready in under 30 minutes!

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp Vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 Large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup Cooked protein (chicken, shrimp, pork, or tofu), diced
  • 1 cup Mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn)
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 3 cups Cooked, cold rice (day-old works best)
  • 2 Green onions, thinly sliced

Sauce/Seasonings

  • 3 tbsp Soy sauce (low sodium recommended)
  • 1 tsp Sesame oil
  • Pinch White pepper (optional)

Instructions

1

Scramble Eggs

Heat 1 tbsp of oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Pour in beaten eggs and cook, scrambling gently, until just cooked through. Remove eggs from skillet and set aside.

2

Cook Protein & Veggies

Add another 1 tbsp of oil to the skillet. Add cooked protein and mixed vegetables. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until heated through and vegetables are tender-crisp. Add minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

3

Fry Rice

Push the protein and veggie mixture to one side of the skillet. Add the remaining 1 tbsp of oil to the empty side. Add the cold rice to the oil, breaking up any clumps with a spatula. Stir-fry the rice for 2-3 minutes until heated through.

4

Combine & Season

Combine the rice with the protein and vegetable mixture. Pour soy sauce and sesame oil over the rice. Add the cooked eggs back into the skillet. Stir everything together well, ensuring the rice is evenly coated and seasoned. Stir-fry for another 1-2 minutes.

5

Finish & Serve

Stir in the sliced green onions and white pepper (if using). Taste and add more soy sauce if needed. Serve immediately.

Chef's Tips

Cold Rice is Key

Use cold, day-old rice. Freshly cooked rice is too moist and will result in mushy fried rice.

High Heat

Use high heat and cook quickly to achieve the characteristic 'wok hei' flavor and prevent sogginess.

Customize It

Feel free to add other veggies like bell peppers, onions, or mushrooms. Adjust soy sauce to your taste.
