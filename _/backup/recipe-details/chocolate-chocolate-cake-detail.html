Decadent Chocolate Cake

Prep Time: 25 mins Cook Time: 30-35 mins Servings: 12 Cuisine: Dessert
Chocolate Cake Dessert Baking

Overview

An intensely rich and moist chocolate cake perfect for any celebration or chocolate craving. This easy-to-make cake features a deep chocolate flavor enhanced by coffee and a luscious chocolate frosting.

Ingredients

Cake

  • 2 cups All-purpose flour
  • 2 cups Granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup Unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 tsp Baking soda
  • 1 tsp Baking powder
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1 cup Buttermilk
  • 1/2 cup Vegetable oil
  • 2 Large eggs
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract
  • 1 cup Hot water or hot brewed coffee

Frosting

  • 1 cup Unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup Unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 cups Powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup Milk
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract

Instructions

1

Preheat & Prepare Pans

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.

2

Mix Dry Ingredients

In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.

3

Add Wet Ingredients

Add buttermilk, oil, eggs, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Beat on medium speed for 2 minutes.

4

Add Hot Liquid

Carefully stir in the hot water or coffee (batter will be thin).

5

Bake

Pour batter evenly into prepared pans. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a wooden skewer inserted into the center comes out clean.

6

Cool

Let cakes cool in pans for 10 minutes before inverting onto a wire rack to cool completely.

7

Make Frosting

Beat softened butter until creamy. Add cocoa powder and beat until combined. Alternately add powdered sugar and milk, beating well after each addition. Stir in vanilla.

8

Frost Cake

Place one cake layer on a serving plate. Spread with frosting. Top with the second layer and frost the top and sides of the cake.

Chef's Tips

Coffee Enhances Chocolate

Using hot coffee instead of water deepens the chocolate flavor.

Room Temperature

Ensure eggs and buttermilk are at room temperature for a smoother batter.

Don't Overmix

Mix batter until just combined; overmixing can make the cake tough.
