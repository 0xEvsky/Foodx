Simple Grilled Salmon

Prep Time: 10 mins Marinating Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 8-12 mins Servings: 4 Cuisine: International
Healthy Seafood Quick Dinner

Overview

This simple grilled salmon recipe is perfect for a quick, healthy, and delicious weeknight meal. The salmon is marinated briefly in a flavorful blend of lemon, garlic, and herbs, then grilled to perfection, resulting in flaky, moist fish with a slightly crispy exterior.

Ingredients

Salmon & Marinade

  • 4 Salmon fillets (6 oz each, skin-on or off)
  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • 2 tbsp Fresh lemon juice
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Dried dill (or 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1/4 tsp Black pepper

For Grilling & Serving

  • As needed Oil for grill grates
  • Optional Lemon wedges, for serving
  • Optional Fresh parsley or dill, chopped for garnish

Instructions

1

Prepare Marinade

In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, dill, salt, and pepper.

2

Marinate Salmon

Place salmon fillets in a shallow dish or a zip-top bag. Pour the marinade over the salmon, ensuring each fillet is coated. Let marinate at room temperature for 15 minutes (do not marinate longer than 30 minutes, as the lemon juice can start to 'cook' the fish).

3

Preheat Grill

Preheat your grill (gas or charcoal) to medium-high heat (around 400-450°F or 200-230°C). Clean the grill grates well and oil them lightly to prevent sticking.

4

Grill Salmon (Skin-Side Down First if Applicable)

Remove salmon from marinade, letting excess drip off. Place salmon on the hot grill. If using skin-on fillets, place them skin-side down first. Close the lid and grill for 4-6 minutes.

5

Flip and Finish Cooking

Carefully flip the salmon fillets. Cook for another 2-6 minutes, depending on the thickness of the fillets and your desired doneness. Salmon is cooked when it flakes easily with a fork or reaches an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C).

6

Rest and Serve

Remove salmon from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve with lemon wedges, if desired.

Chef's Tips

Doneness

Be careful not to overcook salmon. It cooks quickly! Check for flakiness in the thickest part of the fillet.

Prevent Sticking

Ensure grill grates are clean and well-oiled. Don't try to flip the salmon too early; it should release easily when ready.

Variations

Add other herbs like rosemary or thyme to the marinade, or a pinch of red pepper flakes for some heat.
