Beef and Bean Burrito

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 25 mins Servings: 6 Cuisine: Mexican
Mexican Dinner Beef Comfort Food

Overview

These hearty beef and bean burritos feature seasoned ground beef, refried beans, and Mexican-style rice wrapped in a warm flour tortilla with melted cheese. Customize with your favorite toppings for a satisfying meal that's perfect for lunch or dinner. Make a batch and freeze the extras for a quick meal on busy weeknights.

Ingredients

Burrito Filling

  • 1 lb Ground beef
  • 1 Medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 Bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tbsp Taco seasoning
  • 1 can (15 oz) Refried beans
  • 1 cup Cooked rice
  • 1/4 cup Water

Assembly

  • 6 Large flour tortillas (burrito size)
  • 2 cups Shredded cheddar or Mexican blend cheese
  • 1/2 cup Sour cream

Toppings (Optional)

  • 1 Avocado, sliced or guacamole
  • 1/2 cup Chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 cup Pico de gallo or salsa
  • To taste Hot sauce
  • 2 Limes, cut into wedges

Instructions

1

Prepare the Beef Filling

In a large skillet over medium-high heat, brown the ground beef, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. When it's about halfway cooked, add the diced onion and bell pepper. Continue cooking until the beef is no longer pink and the vegetables have softened, about 5-7 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds until fragrant.

2

Season the Mixture

Drain excess fat if necessary. Add the taco seasoning and water to the skillet. Stir well to combine and simmer for 3-4 minutes until the liquid has reduced and the mixture is thick.

3

Warm Tortillas and Beans

Warm the refried beans in a small saucepan or microwave until heated through. Warm the flour tortillas according to package directions or wrap them in damp paper towels and microwave for 30 seconds.

4

Assemble the Burritos

Lay a warm tortilla flat on a clean work surface. Spread about 2 tablespoons of refried beans in the center of the tortilla. Top with about 1/3 cup of the beef mixture, followed by 2-3 tablespoons of cooked rice. Sprinkle with shredded cheese and add a dollop of sour cream.

5

Roll the Burritos

To roll the burrito, fold the sides of the tortilla over the filling, then fold the bottom up and over the filling. Continue rolling tightly away from you until the burrito is sealed. Repeat with remaining tortillas and filling.

6

Toast the Burritos (Optional)

For an extra crispy exterior, place the rolled burritos seam-side down in a clean skillet over medium heat. Cook for 1-2 minutes on each side until golden brown and crispy. Alternatively, you can wrap them in foil to keep them warm and soft.

7

Serve

Serve immediately with your choice of toppings such as guacamole, salsa, additional sour cream, cilantro, and lime wedges.

Chef's Tips

Make Ahead

These burritos can be assembled in advance and refrigerated for up to 2 days or frozen for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly in aluminum foil before storing.

Variations

Use ground turkey or chicken instead of beef for a lighter option. For vegetarian burritos, substitute the meat with more beans or add roasted vegetables.

Reheating Frozen Burritos

To reheat frozen burritos, remove foil, wrap in a damp paper towel, and microwave for 2-3 minutes, flipping halfway through. For a crispy exterior, finish in a 350°F oven for 5-10 minutes.
