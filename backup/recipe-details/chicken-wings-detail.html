Crispy Baked Chicken Wings

Prep Time: 10 mins Cook Time: 45-50 mins Servings: 4-6 (Appetizer) Cuisine: American
Chicken Appetizer Baked Game Day

Overview

Achieve perfectly crispy chicken wings without deep frying! This baked method uses a secret ingredient (baking powder) to ensure super crispy skin, ready to be tossed in your favorite sauce or enjoyed plain.

Ingredients

Wings & Coating

  • 3 lbs Chicken wings, split into drumettes and flats
  • 1 tbsp Baking powder (aluminum-free)
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp Garlic powder

Optional Sauce (Buffalo Example)

  • 1/2 cup Hot sauce (e.g., Frank's RedHot)
  • 1/4 cup Unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tbsp White vinegar
  • 1/4 tsp Garlic powder

Instructions

1

Dry Wings

Pat chicken wings thoroughly dry with paper towels. This is crucial for crispiness.

2

Coat Wings

In a large bowl, whisk together baking powder, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Add dried wings and toss until evenly coated.

3

Arrange on Rack

Place a wire rack inside a foil-lined baking sheet. Arrange coated wings in a single layer on the wire rack, ensuring they don't touch.

4

Bake Low then High

Place baking sheet in a preheated 250°F (120°C) oven on a lower rack. Bake for 25 minutes. Increase oven temperature to 425°F (220°C) and move wings to an upper rack. Bake for another 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden brown and crispy.

5

Make Sauce (Optional)

While wings finish baking, whisk together sauce ingredients in a small bowl.

6

Toss and Serve

Let wings rest for 5 minutes after baking. Transfer to a large bowl, add sauce (if using), and toss to coat. Serve immediately with celery sticks and blue cheese or ranch dressing.

Chef's Tips

Dryness is Key

The drier the wings before coating, the crispier they will be. Let them air dry in the fridge on the rack for an hour if possible.

Baking Powder NOT Soda

Ensure you use baking powder, not baking soda. Baking powder helps draw out moisture and aids browning.

Don't Sauce Too Early

Toss wings in sauce just before serving to maintain maximum crispiness.
