Simple Homemade Pasta

Prep Time: 45 mins Resting Time: 30 mins Cook Time: 3-5 mins Servings: 4 Cuisine: Italian
Pasta Italian Homemade Basics

Overview

Discover the joy of making fresh pasta from scratch with this simple recipe. Using just a few basic ingredients, you can create tender, delicious pasta that elevates any sauce. It's easier than you think!

Ingredients

Pasta Dough

  • 2 cups All-purpose flour (or "00" flour)
  • 3 Large eggs
  • 1 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • Extra flour for dusting

Instructions

1

Make Dough

Mound flour on a clean work surface. Make a well in the center. Crack eggs into the well, add olive oil and salt. Whisk eggs with a fork, gradually incorporating flour from the sides until a shaggy dough forms.

2

Knead Dough

Bring the dough together and knead on a lightly floured surface for 8-10 minutes, until smooth and elastic.

3

Rest Dough

Wrap the dough tightly in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes.

4

Roll Out Dough

Divide dough into quarters. Working with one piece at a time (keep others covered), flatten the dough. Use a pasta machine or rolling pin to roll it out thinly. If using a machine, start at the widest setting and gradually decrease, folding the dough as needed, until desired thickness is reached. If using a rolling pin, roll as thin as possible on a floured surface.

5

Cut Pasta

Cut the dough sheets into your desired shape (e.g., fettuccine, tagliatelle) using the pasta machine cutter or by hand with a sharp knife. Dust cut pasta lightly with flour.

6

Cook Pasta

Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Add fresh pasta and cook for 3-5 minutes, or until al dente. Drain immediately.

7

Serve

Toss cooked pasta with your favorite sauce and serve immediately.

Chef's Tips

Flour Type

"00" flour creates a silkier pasta, but all-purpose works well too.

Dough Consistency

If dough is too dry, add a teaspoon of water; if too sticky, add a tablespoon of flour.

Drying/Storing

You can cook pasta immediately, let it dry slightly on a rack, or freeze for later use.
