Classic French Macarons

Prep Time: 1 hour Resting Time: 30-60 mins Cook Time: 15-18 mins Yield: 20-24 macarons Cuisine: French
Dessert Baking Gluten-Free Challenging

Overview

Master the art of French macarons with this detailed recipe. These delicate almond meringue cookies are sandwiched with a luscious filling (buttercream shown), known for their smooth shells, ruffled 'feet', and chewy texture. While they require precision, the stunning results are incredibly rewarding.

Ingredients

Macaron Shells

  • 1 cup (100g) Almond flour, finely ground
  • 3/4 cup (100g) Powdered sugar
  • 2 Large egg whites (about 70g), room temperature
  • 1/4 tsp Cream of tartar
  • 1/4 cup (50g) Granulated sugar
  • Optional Gel food coloring

Buttercream Filling (Example)

  • 1/2 cup (113g) Unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups (180g) Powdered sugar, sifted
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract
  • 1-2 tbsp Milk or cream

Instructions

1

Prepare Dry Ingredients

Sift almond flour and powdered sugar together into a large bowl 2-3 times to remove lumps and aerate. Discard any large almond pieces.

2

Whip Egg Whites

In a separate, very clean bowl (preferably glass or metal), beat egg whites with an electric mixer on medium speed until foamy. Add cream of tartar. Continue beating, gradually adding granulated sugar one tablespoon at a time, until stiff, glossy peaks form (meringue should hold its shape).

3

Add Coloring (Optional)

If using gel food coloring, add it during the last minute of whipping the meringue.

4

Macaronage (Folding)

Add about 1/3 of the sifted dry ingredients to the meringue. Gently fold using a rubber spatula until just combined. Add the remaining dry ingredients in two batches, folding carefully after each addition. Continue folding (pressing batter against the side of the bowl, then scooping) until the batter flows like slow-moving lava or forms a ribbon when lifted. Be careful not to overmix.

5

Pipe Shells

Transfer batter to a piping bag fitted with a round tip (e.g., Wilton 12). Line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats. Pipe 1.5-inch circles onto the prepared sheets, leaving space between them. Firmly tap the baking sheets on the counter several times to release air bubbles.

6

Rest Shells

Let the piped shells rest at room temperature for 30-60 minutes (or longer in humid conditions) until a skin forms. They should not feel sticky when lightly touched.

7

Bake

Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Bake one sheet at a time for 15-18 minutes, rotating halfway through. The shells should have developed 'feet' and should not wobble when gently nudged. Let cool completely on the baking sheet before removing.

8

Prepare Filling

While shells cool, prepare the buttercream (or your desired filling). Beat softened butter until creamy. Gradually add sifted powdered sugar, beating until smooth. Mix in vanilla extract and milk/cream until desired consistency is reached.

9

Assemble Macarons

Pair cooled macaron shells by size. Pipe or spread filling onto the flat side of one shell and gently sandwich with another shell.

10

Mature

For best texture, place assembled macarons in an airtight container in the refrigerator for at least 24 hours before serving. Bring to room temperature before enjoying.

Chef's Tips

Weigh Ingredients

Using a kitchen scale for accuracy, especially for almond flour, sugar, and egg whites, is highly recommended for consistent results.

Avoid Liquid Coloring

Use gel or powder food coloring, as liquid coloring can add too much moisture and affect the meringue.

Mind the Humidity

Macarons are sensitive to humidity. Avoid making them on very rainy or humid days if possible, as it can affect drying time and texture.
