Classic Cheesecake

Prep Time: 30 mins Cook Time: 55 mins Cooling Time: 4+ hours Servings: 12 Cuisine: Dessert
Dessert Baking Cream Cheese Special Occasion

Overview

A rich and creamy classic cheesecake with a graham cracker crust. This indulgent dessert is perfect for special occasions or when you're craving something truly decadent. The smooth, velvety texture and balanced sweetness will make this a crowd favorite!

Ingredients

Crust

  • 1 1/2 cups Graham cracker crumbs (about 10 crackers)
  • 5 tbsp Unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup Granulated sugar
  • 1/4 tsp Salt

Filling

  • 32 oz (4 blocks) Cream cheese, room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups Granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup Sour cream, room temperature
  • 2 tsp Vanilla extract
  • 4 Large eggs, room temperature

Optional Topping

  • 1 cup Sour cream
  • 2 tbsp Granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp Vanilla extract
  • As needed Fresh berries for garnish

Instructions

1

Prepare Pan & Oven

Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Wrap the outside of a 9-inch springform pan with a double layer of heavy-duty aluminum foil, ensuring it comes up at least 2 inches on all sides.

2

Make Crust

In a medium bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, sugar, and salt. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom and about 1 inch up the sides of the prepared springform pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then remove and cool while preparing the filling.

3

Make Filling

In a large mixing bowl, beat cream cheese on medium speed until completely smooth, about 4 minutes. Add sugar and beat until well incorporated, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed. Add sour cream and vanilla, beat until smooth. Add eggs one at a time, beating just until incorporated after each addition. Do not overmix.

4

Prepare Water Bath

Place the foil-wrapped springform pan in a larger roasting pan. Pour the filling into the springform pan, smoothing the top. Add hot water to the roasting pan until it reaches about halfway up the sides of the springform pan.

5

Bake

Bake for 55-65 minutes until the edges are set but the center still has a slight jiggle when gently shaken. Turn off the oven, crack the door open, and let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour.

6

Add Optional Topping

If using the sour cream topping, whisk together sour cream, sugar, and vanilla. Spread over the warm cheesecake and return to the oven for 10 minutes. Skip this step if not using the topping.

7

Chill

Remove cheesecake from water bath, remove foil, and cool completely on a wire rack. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

8

Serve

Run a knife around the edge of the pan before removing the sides. Garnish with fresh berries if desired, and serve chilled.

Chef's Tips

Room Temperature

Ensure all dairy ingredients are at room temperature for a smooth filling without lumps.

Water Bath

Don't skip the water bath - it prevents cracking by providing gentle, even heat and humidity.

Cooling Slowly

Slow cooling in the oven prevents dramatic temperature changes that cause cracking.
